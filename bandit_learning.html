My take of general bandit learning

Besides the default linkage with the multi-armed bandit problem, the term bandit learning can refer to a much broader class of problems. Bandit learning can refer to online leaning problems where feedbacks depend on the choice of agent and are only revealed after agent's decisions. This definition of the term ''bandit'' is often used in contrast to full-feedback online learning (e.g., this book by Cesa-Bianchi and Lugosi). Problems that fit into this definition of ''bandit'' include (certain aspects of) multi-armed bandits, reinforcement learning, learning to control, stochastic approximation, optimization, etc. For example, the Q-learning algorithm, as an online algorithm where feedbacks (rewards) depend on the choice of agent and are only revealed after agent's decisions, can be viewed as a bandit learning algorithm.
