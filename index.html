Tianyu Wang

I'm an assistant professor at Shanghai Center for Mathematical Science (SCMS), Fudan University. Before joining Fudan, I obtained my doctorate degree from Duke University.

Research interests: applied math, machine learning, and applications of math. For applied math, in the broadest scope, I am interested in approximation for applied analysis, and computation for applied geometry and applied topology. In a down-to-earth scope, I am currently interested in stochastic estimation that respects local geometric structure (and fast stochastic computation for persistence diagrams of filtered non-oriented simplicial complexes over Noetherian rings). For machine learning, I am interested in mathematically accessible but non-trivial models that capture real-world decision processes or data structures. Examples include the multi-armed bandit problems and sometimes data-driven optimization. For applications of math, I am interested in developing principled, accessible, well-performing methods and algorithms for problems of strong practical motivation.
